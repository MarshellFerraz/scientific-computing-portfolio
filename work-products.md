# Substantial Digital and Documented Work Products

This document summarizes substantial digital and documented work products developed across scientific computing, biomedical modeling, computational chemistry, engineering, education, technical documentation, and AI-assisted scientific evaluation.

## 1. Computational and Mathematical Biomedical Modeling

Developed computational and mathematical frameworks to model chronic wound biology, diabetic foot ulcer progression, oxidative stress, carbonyl stress, glycation, inflammation, biomaterial–tissue interactions, and therapeutic prioritization.

These work products involved literature review, mechanistic abstraction, variable definition, mathematical formulation, biomarker interpretation, model documentation, and iterative refinement of biological assumptions.

## 2. Python-Based Scientific Computing Workflows

Developed Python-based workflows for scientific modeling, simulation, data interpretation, kinetic analysis, curve fitting, biomarker simulation, and decision-support applications in biomedical and translational research.

These workflows support reproducible scientific reasoning, numerical interpretation, graphical visualization, and structured analysis of experimental, computational, and literature-derived information.

## 3. Computational Chemistry and Biophysics Workflows

Prepared documented workflows involving ligand preparation, protein target preparation, molecular docking, molecular dynamics planning, protein–ligand interaction analysis, fluorescence-guided binding interpretation, antiglycation mechanisms, and bioactive-compound prioritization.

These workflows support mechanistic interpretation of ligand–protein binding, structure–activity reasoning, and computational evidence generation for biomedical research.

## 6. Teaching, Assessment, and Engineering Education Materials
